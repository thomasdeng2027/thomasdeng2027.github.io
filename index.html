Lunar Autonomy Challenge

Built an AI-driven arc-based path-planning system for lunar rover navigation. Developed a full perception-to-planning pipeline integrating Language Segment-Anything (LangSAM) for rock segmentation, Depth Anything V2 for depth estimation, stereo-based disparity for geometric reconstruction, and Dubins-model concatenated arc trajectories for safe local planning. Enabled the rover to detect obstacles, estimate terrain geometry, and follow global waypoints by repeatedly selecting collision-free arcs and executing real-time replanning. Contributed to NAVLab’s end-to-end autonomy stack spanning perception, localization, mapping, and control.
